# Home Wireframe

# Project Trident Homepage Wireframe & Layout Structure

---

# Introduction

This document defines the structural homepage layout for Project Trident.

The homepage is intended to function as:
- a trust surface,
- a philosophy surface,
- and a technology education entry point.

The homepage should not behave like:
- an e-commerce landing page,
- a marketplace feed,
- or an affiliate recommendation wall.

Its primary role is to help users understand:
- why the platform exists,
- what problem it solves,
- and how technology-first discovery works.

---

# Core Homepage Objectives

The homepage should:

- establish credibility,
- explain the platform philosophy,
- educate users about technology evolution,
- and guide users into structured discovery.

The page should prioritize:
- understanding,
- transparency,
- and trust

before:
- recommendations,
- comparisons,
- or product discovery.

---

# Homepage Emotional Direction

The homepage should feel:
- calm,
- trustworthy,
- technical,
- and educational.

It should avoid:
- clutter,
- aggressive recommendation pressure,
- artificial urgency,
- and marketplace-style visual overload.

The objective is:
- clarity over stimulation.

---

# Global Header (Navbar)

Every public page shares the V1 header defined in **[Navigation Flow — V1 Global Header](02-navigation-flow.md#v1-global-header-navbar)**.

```txt
[Logo]  Explore Technologies | Discover Products | Compare | How We Review | Transparency   [Search]
```

- The homepage **hero** may repeat key CTAs (e.g. Explore Technologies, Learn How We Review) but must not replace or contradict the global navbar.
- Trust links in page body (methodology, rankings, ethics) should align with **How We Review** and **Transparency** nav destinations.

---

# High-Level Homepage Structure

The homepage is organized into the following major sections:

1. Hero Section
2. Trust & Credibility Strip
3. Technology Spotlight
4. Technology Landscape
5. Technology Evolution Awareness
6. Buying Mistake Education
7. Validation Methodology Preview
8. Technology-Based Discovery
9. Transparency & Ethics
10. Footer

---

# Section 1 — Hero Section

## Purpose

Immediately explain:
- the platform mission,
- the core problem,
- and the platform identity.

---

## Primary Message Direction

Example positioning direction:
> “Don’t buy outdated tech just because it has more ratings.”

The hero should communicate:
- technology-first thinking,
- independent validation,
- and future-proof decision support.

---

## Hero Components

### Headline
Clear philosophy-driven statement.

### Supporting Description
Short explanation of:
- marketplace limitations,
- and technology evolution problems.

### Primary Actions
Examples:
- Explore Technologies
- Learn How We Review

---

## Hero Design Philosophy

The hero should avoid:
- hype language,
- sales messaging,
- discount emphasis,
- and aggressive calls to action.

The objective is:
- trust establishment.

# Homepage Information Hierarchy

The homepage should guide users through:

1. Problem awareness
2. Platform philosophy
3. Trust establishment
4. Technology understanding
5. Discovery preparation
6. Exploration entry points

Products should appear only after:
- educational framing exists.

---

# Visual Design Principles

The homepage should prioritize:
- whitespace,
- readability,
- information clarity,
- and visual calmness.

The page should avoid:
- dense product grids,
- excessive animations,
- and advertisement-like visual patterns.

---

# Long-Term Homepage Role

As Project Trident evolves, the homepage should continue functioning as:
- a trust gateway,
- a philosophy gateway,
- and a technology education surface.

Even with future expansion, the homepage should remain centered around:
- understanding,
- transparency,
- and credibility.

---

# Anti-Goals

The homepage should avoid becoming:
- a marketplace clone,
- an advertisement surface,
- a trend feed,
- or a conversion-optimized landing page.

If the homepage starts prioritizing:
- clicks,
- engagement metrics,
- or monetization visibility

over:
- clarity and trust,

the platform risks losing its identity.

---

# Final Principle

> The homepage should help users think more clearly about technology before encouraging them to explore products.
