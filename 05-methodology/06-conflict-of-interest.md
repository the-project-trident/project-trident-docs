# Conflict of Interest Policy

# Project Trident Conflict of Interest & Independence Policy

---

# Introduction

Project Trident is built around:
- trust,
- transparency,
- independent evaluation,
- and explainable recommendations.

Conflicts of interest can weaken:
- recommendation integrity,
- platform credibility,
- and user trust.

This document defines the principles and policies intended to help Project Trident:
- identify,
- disclose,
- manage,
- and minimize conflicts of interest.

The objective is not to claim perfect neutrality.

The objective is to:
- remain transparent,
- structurally independent,
- and ethically accountable.

---

# Core Philosophy

Project Trident believes that:
> hidden influence damages trust faster than honest disclosure.

Conflicts of interest are not always avoidable.

However:
- undisclosed influence,
- hidden incentives,
- and opaque recommendation behavior

are unacceptable within a trust-oriented platform.

Transparency is mandatory.

---

# What Is a Conflict of Interest?

A conflict of interest exists when:
- financial,
- personal,
- commercial,
- or relational incentives

could reasonably influence:
- evaluations,
- recommendations,
- verification outcomes,
- or visibility decisions.

Conflicts may be:
- direct,
- indirect,
- actual,
- or perceived.

All forms should be treated seriously.

---

# Types of Potential Conflicts

Potential conflicts may include:

- financial relationships,
- sponsorship arrangements,
- affiliate incentives,
- reviewer relationships,
- vendor partnerships,
- personal ownership interests,
- employment relationships,
- or external pressure.

The platform should remain aware that:
- perceived conflicts can damage trust even when behavior remains technically independent.

---

# Recommendation Independence

Recommendations should never be influenced by:
- payments,
- sponsorships,
- vendor pressure,
- affiliate commissions,
- or popularity incentives.

Recommendation logic should remain:
- transparent,
- explainable,
- and evidence-driven.

Trust systems should remain structurally protected.

---

# Reviewer Independence Expectations

Reviewers should avoid:
- undisclosed incentives,
- hidden vendor relationships,
- and biased evaluation behavior.

If relevant relationships or conflicts exist, they should be:
- disclosed clearly,
- and documented appropriately.

Reviewer trust depends on:
- transparency,
- and accountability.

---

# Vendor Relationship Boundaries

Project Trident may eventually interact with vendors for:
- product submissions,
- technical clarification,
- and documentation access.

However:
- vendors should not control:
  - platform verdicts,
  - recommendation rankings,
  - or verification outcomes.

The platform should maintain:
- structural separation between participation and trust authority.

---

# Sponsored Content Restrictions

Project Trident aims to avoid:
- hidden sponsored influence,
- disguised advertising,
- and recommendation manipulation.

If sponsored content exists in the future, it should:
- remain clearly labeled,
- visually distinct,
- and structurally separated from:
  - platform verification,
  - trust systems,
  - and recommendation logic.

Users should never confuse:
- sponsorship,
with:
- independent evaluation.

---

# Affiliate Relationship Transparency

If affiliate systems are introduced in the future:
- affiliate relationships should be disclosed clearly.

Affiliate incentives should never:
- determine rankings,
- override verification outcomes,
- or influence trust systems.

The platform should avoid:
- affiliate-first recommendation behavior.

Trust should remain primary.

---

# Financial Independence Philosophy

Project Trident should avoid becoming:
- financially dependent on specific vendors,
- or structurally vulnerable to sponsor pressure.

Excessive dependency increases risk of:
- recommendation compromise,
- self-censorship,
- and trust erosion.

Long-term sustainability should support:
- independence,
not:
- influence vulnerability.

---

# Handling Product Samples & Access

If reviewers receive:
- review units,
- temporary access,
- or vendor-provided hardware,

the platform should maintain:
- disclosure transparency,
- evaluation independence,
- and methodology consistency.

Receiving access should never guarantee:
- positive outcomes,
- or favorable visibility.

---

# Recommendation Accountability

Major recommendation decisions should remain:
- reviewable,
- explainable,
- and historically accountable.

The platform should avoid:
- hidden recommendation manipulation,
- silent ranking changes,
- and opaque prioritization behavior.

Users should understand:
- why products are surfaced,
- and what evidence supports recommendations.

---

# Community Influence Caution

Community popularity should not automatically determine:
- recommendation authority,
- or verification outcomes.

Project Trident should avoid:
- crowd-driven trust systems,
- and engagement-based authority structures.

Community input is valuable but should remain:
- one signal among many.

---

# Human Accountability Matters

Project Trident should avoid:
- fully opaque algorithmic authority,
- and unreviewable recommendation systems.

Human oversight remains essential for:
- contextual interpretation,
- trade-off evaluation,
- and ethical accountability.

Trust systems require responsible stewardship.

---

# Documentation & Auditability

Conflict-related decisions should remain:
- documented,
- reviewable,
- and historically traceable.

The platform should support:
- transparency audits,
- recommendation accountability,
- and methodology visibility.

Historical accountability improves trust consistency.

---

# Long-Term Independence Goal

Project Trident aims to become:
- difficult to manipulate,
- structurally transparent,
- and credibility-oriented.

The platform’s reputation should emerge from:
- repeated evidence of independence,
- and long-term ethical consistency.

---

# Anti-Goals

The platform should avoid becoming:
- sponsor-controlled,
- affiliate-driven,
- popularity-manipulated,
- or commercially dependent on recommendation influence.

If monetization begins controlling:
- visibility,
- verification,
- or recommendations,

the platform risks losing:
- trust,
- credibility,
- and long-term viability.

---

# Final Principle

> Trust systems fail when users cannot distinguish between independent evaluation and hidden influence.

Project Trident exists to keep that distinction visible and protected.
