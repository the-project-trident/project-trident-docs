# ADR-002 — No Paid Rankings Policy

# Status

Accepted

---

# Date

2026-05-17

---

# Context

Many modern discovery platforms and marketplaces are heavily influenced by:
- sponsored placements,
- paid visibility systems,
- affiliate incentives,
- and commercial ranking manipulation.

These systems often blur the distinction between:
- independent recommendations,
- advertisements,
- and monetized visibility.

As a result:
- users struggle to determine what is genuinely recommended,
- trust systems become opaque,
- and recommendation integrity weakens over time.

Project Trident is intended to function as:
- a trust-oriented technology intelligence platform.

The platform’s long-term value depends heavily on:
- credibility,
- recommendation transparency,
- and explainable trust systems.

Therefore:
- recommendation independence becomes foundational infrastructure rather than optional policy.

---

# Decision

Project Trident will adopt:
- a no paid rankings policy.

Products will not receive:
- ranking advantages,
- recommendation boosts,
- or trust visibility improvements

because of:
- sponsorships,
- affiliate relationships,
- vendor payments,
- or commercial agreements.

Recommendation systems should remain:
- evidence-driven,
- technology-aware,
- and explainable.

If monetization systems exist in the future, they must remain:
- clearly disclosed,
- structurally separated,
- and operationally isolated from:
  - recommendation systems,
  - verification systems,
  - and trust infrastructure.

---

# Implications

This decision strongly affects:

- monetization strategy,
- vendor relationships,
- recommendation architecture,
- UX systems,
- and trust infrastructure design.

The platform must maintain:
- clear separation between:
  - commercial systems,
  - and trust systems.

This also requires:
- strong transparency systems,
- accountability infrastructure,
- and auditability mechanisms.

---

# Benefits

Potential benefits include:

- stronger long-term user trust,
- clearer recommendation integrity,
- improved platform credibility,
- reduced manipulation risk,
- and stronger platform differentiation.

The platform becomes:
- trust-oriented,
rather than:
- advertisement-driven.

---

# Risks

Potential risks include:

- slower monetization opportunities,
- reduced short-term revenue flexibility,
- increased operational difficulty,
- and stronger pressure to develop sustainable ethical business models.

Maintaining independence may become:
- financially challenging at scale.

---

# Alternatives Considered

## Sponsored Ranking Systems

Allow vendors to:
- purchase visibility,
- or ranking priority.

Rejected because:
- it weakens trust systems,
- reduces recommendation integrity,
- and creates long-term credibility risks.

---

## Hidden Affiliate Optimization

Optimize recommendations indirectly for:
- affiliate conversion opportunities.

Rejected because:
- it creates opaque recommendation incentives,
- weakens transparency,
- and damages user trust.

---

## Full Marketplace Advertising Model

Adopt:
- advertisement-heavy monetization,
- and marketplace-style promotional systems.

Rejected because:
- it conflicts directly with:
  - trust-oriented discovery,
  - educational UX,
  - and recommendation transparency.

---

# Long-Term Consequences

This decision strongly shapes:
- platform identity,
- user expectations,
- monetization constraints,
- and long-term strategic positioning.

Project Trident positions itself as:
- a credibility-oriented platform,
not:
- a visibility marketplace.

This creates:
- stronger trust potential,
but also:
- greater responsibility for sustainable ethical growth models.

---

# Enforcement Principles

To preserve this decision:
- recommendation systems should remain auditable,
- sponsorship relationships should remain visible,
- and trust systems should remain structurally isolated from monetization systems.

Users should always understand:
- what is independently recommended,
- and what is commercial participation.

---

# Final Principle

> Recommendation visibility should be earned through evidence, technology relevance, and trust quality — not purchased through commercial influence.
